The Paleolithic Era

In the Paleolithic period (roughly 2.5 million years ago to 10,000 B.C.),
early humans lived in caves or simple huts and were hunters and gatherers.
They used basic stone and bone tools, as well as crude stone axes, for hunting birds and wild animals.
They cooked their prey, including woolly mammoths, deer and bison, using controlled fire.
They also fished and collected berries, fruit and nuts.
Ancient humans in the Paleolithic period were also the first to leave behind art.
They used combinations of minerals, ochres, burnt bone meal and charcoal mixed into
water, blood, animal fats and tree saps to etch humans, animals and signs.
They also carved small figurines from stones, clay, bones and antlers.
The end of this period marked the end of the last Ice Age,
which resulted in the extinction of many large mammals and rising sea levels and
climate change that eventually caused man to migrate.
